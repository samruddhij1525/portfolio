01 — Brief
The Opportunity

Zudio is India's fastest-growing value fashion brand under Tata Group — yet it has zero digital shopping presence. With e-commerce projected to hit USD 200 billion by 2026, this gap represents a massive untapped market.

Brand
Zudio

Tata Group's Trent Limited value fashion retail chain targeting youth and families across tier 1 & 2 Indian cities. Known for affordable, trend-driven clothing in a premium-feeling retail environment.

Project
Design a Mobile App

From research to high-fidelity wireframes — covering discovery, shopping, virtual try-on, store locator, and omnichannel features for Zudio's first digital shopping experience.

02 — Problem Statement
Defining the Gap

Through interviews and market research, a clear accessibility gap emerged. Zudio's physical-store-only model fails to serve its growing digital audience.

"Users browse in-store but lack knowledge of stock availability — while remote users cannot access the brand at all."
Zudio's current model emphasizes physical stores, excluding customers outside store catchments and failing to capture digital-first engagement. Competitors like Myntra and Ajio dominate with superior UX, while Zudio remains inaccessible beyond its store walls.
Pain Points Identified
No real-time stock visibility before visiting the store
40+ min travel wasted on out-of-stock trips
No wishlist or save-for-later feature exists
No size guide or product details accessible online
Competitors (Myntra, Ajio) are far more convenient
How Might We…
How might we bring Zudio's inventory online?
How might we enable discovery without a store visit?
How might we reduce returns via better sizing tools?
How might we create a frictionless checkout?
How might we connect online & offline shopping?

Ideation — Key Innovation Triggers
Trust → Photo verification of packed items before dispatch ensures quality
Speed → Guest Checkout & UPI integration — remove the "Login Wall" barrier
Local → Localised store inventory visibility so users check before they travel
Ship from Store → Replace shipping-only with utilising offline store inventory
Event Picks → Festival & birthday-based recommendations drive impulse buys
Outfit Checker → Full mix & match replaces basic measurement options
In-Store Mode → App acts as inventory checker for physical shoppers too
Browse First → Full catalogue before signup — no forced login at start
04 — Competitor Analysis
The Landscape

Mapping Zudio's competitors by segment, strengths, weaknesses — and the strategic insights that shaped our design decisions.

Westside
SegmentMid-value curated fashion
StrengthsTrendy in-house labels, better quality, strong store aesthetics
WeaknessHigher prices, not ultra-budget friendly
Indirect competitor
Key InsightZudio can win on price but needs to match Westside's premium in-app feel to be seen as aspirational, not cheap.
Trends (Reliance)
SegmentValue fashion
StrengthsHuge store network, low prices, strong private labels
WeaknessInconsistent quality and store experience, weak digital UX
Direct competitor
Key InsightNeither Trends nor Zudio has a strong app — first-mover advantage in the value fashion app space is still open.
Max Fashion
SegmentValue fashion
StrengthsGood quality-for-price, strong Western wear, wide assortment
WeaknessSlightly higher prices, slower trend updates
Same youth + family segment
Key InsightMax has a working app — Zudio must differentiate with features Max lacks: try-on, mix & match, and real-time store stock.
Pantaloons
SegmentAffordable to mid-value lifestyle
StrengthsStrong ethnic wear, wide mix, trusted brand name
WeaknessHigher prices than typical value fashion
Partial overlap — casual/ethnic
Key InsightPantaloons' filtering & loyalty features can be benchmarked; Zudio's edge is a younger, more impulsive audience who want speed over points.
Overall Findings
Gap in Market
No value-fashion brand has a best-in-class app. The space is wide open for Zudio to own.
UX is the Differentiator
Higher UX quality directly correlates with market share. An intuitive app creates a real competitive moat.
Omnichannel Wins
Bridging physical stores with a digital app through click & collect and live stock visibility is completely untapped.

Heuristic Evaluation — Nielsen's 10 Principles
Visibility of Status
Location, cart count & outfit match % always visible to the user
Real World Match
Mix & Match mirrors how people actually dress (top–bottom–shoes)
User Control
Edit cart, remove items, adjust profile without breaking flow
Consistency
Uniform card styles, typography, and buttons across all screens
Recognition over Recall
Image-first selection lets users choose visually, not from memory
Aesthetic & Minimal
Large product images, clean cards, attention stays on the clothes
